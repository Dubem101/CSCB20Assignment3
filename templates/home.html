Course Description


Design, analysis, implementation and comparison of efficient data structures for common abstract data types. Priority queues: heaps and mergeable heaps. Dictionaries: balanced binary search trees, B-trees, hashing. Amortization: data structures for managing dynamic tables and disjoint sets. Data structures for representing graphs. Graph searches.
Exclusion CSC263H, CSC265H, (CSCC78H)
Prerequisite CSCB36H or (CSCB38H) & [CGPA 2.75 or enrolment in a CSC subject POSt]

Course Team Information

Dr. Bretscher joined the department in 2004 after completing a Ph.D in Graph Theory at the University of Toronto and Master's and Bachelor's degrees at Queen's University. After teaching a course as a graduate student, Anna came to the realization that teaching students theoretical concepts is where her passion lies. In particular, she loves the challenge of simplifying difficult concepts and the reward of successfully teaching new topics.
